# Kanban board

The Kanban board is KanBlam's centerpiece. Tasks live in columns by
**stage**; you move them right as work progresses.

![The Kanban board with Stratos-1 mission tasks spread across Ideas, In Progress, On Hold, Completed, and Cancelled columns](/images/docs/kanban.png)

## The default stages

Out of the box your workspace has five stages:

- **Ideas** — captured but not committed to. The parking lot.
- **In Progress** — actively worked on.
- **On Hold** — started, then blocked on something outside your
  control. A card in On Hold should say *what it's waiting on* in its
  notes — "supplier out of stock" beats a silent parked card.
- **Completed** — done. Cards collapse here.
- **Cancelled** — abandoned without completing. Keeping these visible
  (rather than deleting) preserves the decision: *"Evaluate GSM backup
  tracker — cancelled, no coverage above 2 km."*

Admins can rename or reorder stages from the database directly today
(a Settings UI for stage editing is on the roadmap). Talk to your
workspace admin if you need different stages.

## Reading a card

Each card shows the task name, its project code, priority, due date,
tags, assignee avatar, and — when the task has subtasks — a progress
bar. Glyphs mark ★ Important, ⏱ Urgent and 🔁 Recurring tasks, so a
glance at a column tells you not just *how much* work is there but
*what kind*.

## Moving cards

**With the mouse:** click the **grip handle** (the dotted vertical bar
top-right of each card) and drag to another column or position.
Releasing snaps the card into its new home. The change saves
immediately — no Save button.

**With the keyboard:** tab into a card's grip handle, press
`Space` to pick up, arrow keys to move, `Space` again to drop, `Esc`
to cancel. Useful for moving multiple cards quickly without
reaching for the trackpad.

**Click anywhere else on the card** to open it in the side drawer for
editing.

## Filtering the board

The global filter chips in the top bar apply here:

- **Project** — show only one project's tasks
- **Assignee** — show only tasks assigned to one person
- **Tags** — multi-select; tasks with at least one matching tag stay
  visible
- **Quadrant** — show only Important & Urgent, etc.
- **Hide completed** — collapses the Completed and Cancelled columns
  to summaries

## Swim lanes

Above the board, the **Lane** dropdown splits the board horizontally
by a second axis:

- **None** — the default, one row of columns.
- **By project** — each lane is a project. This is the view for
  running several workstreams at once: one glance shows the payload
  build humming along while recovery prep hasn't started.
- **By assignee** — one lane per person. The team stand-up view:
  who's overloaded, who's blocked, who's free.
- **By tag** — lanes per tag; a task with two tags appears in both
  lanes.

![The Kanban board grouped into per-project swim lanes](/images/docs/kanban-swimlanes.png)

Lanes can be collapsed individually — click the chevron on the lane
row header to fold a lane down to a summary count. The board
remembers your collapsed lanes between visits.

## Recipes

**Weekly triage.** Filter to nothing, look at **Ideas** only. Anything
that's been there over a month either gets a due date and a drag to
In Progress, or an honest trip to Cancelled. Ideas is a parking lot,
not a landfill.

**Limit work in progress.** If your In Progress column doesn't fit on
one screen, you're not doing that work — you're storing it. Drag the
excess back to Ideas and feel nothing.

**Run a multi-project week.** Lane by project, collapse the lanes
you're not touching today, and work the remaining lanes left to
right.
