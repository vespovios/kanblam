# Recurring tasks

Recurring tasks repeat on a cadence — daily, weekly, monthly, or a
custom pattern. KanBlam handles them through a **template-and-instance**
model: you describe the recurrence once, KanBlam generates concrete
task instances ahead of time so they appear in your Kanban, Calendar
and Tasks list like any other task.

## Creating a recurring task

In the New Task dialog (or from Quick Add), toggle the **Repeat**
section open. Pick a preset:

![The new-task dialog with the Repeat dropdown for setting a recurrence](/images/docs/task-create-dialog.png)

- **Does not repeat** — a normal one-off task.
- **Every weekday** — Monday through Friday.
- **Daily** — every N days.
- **Weekly** — every N weeks, optionally on specific weekdays.
- **Monthly** — every N months on the same day-of-month.
- **Custom** — combine the dimensions above.

The **Start date** controls when the first instance appears. **End
date** (optional) caps the series.

## How instances are generated

KanBlam runs a background cron every five minutes. For each active
recurring template it materialises:

- All occurrences from the **rolling high-water mark** (`lastGeneratedDate
  + 1 day`, or the start date the first time) up to a window end.
- The window end is **`max(today + 14 days, the Nth upcoming occurrence)`**.
  For daily / weekly rules the day-count bound wins; for quarterly or
  yearly rules the occurrence-count floor ensures you always see your
  next handful of instances regardless of how far apart they are.

You don't need to think about any of this. Open Kanban, your weekly
recurring task is sitting in its stage for next Monday — on the demo
workspace, the Saturday *"APRS net check-in"* appears in Ideas with a
🔁 glyph every week, without anyone creating it.

## Editing or deleting a recurring instance

Click a recurring task to open it in the drawer. Make a change and
hit Save — KanBlam asks **which instances** the change should apply to:

- **This task** — only this single occurrence.
- **This and following** — this occurrence and every future one.
  Splits the series at this date.
- **All tasks** — every instance of the series (past and future).

Same prompt on **Delete**. Choose carefully: "All tasks" deletes the
entire series including the underlying template.

## What propagates vs what doesn't

When you edit a series with **All** or **Following** scope, the
template-defining fields propagate to existing instances:

- ✅ Name, description, priority, importance flag, urgency flag, tags

The **work-state** fields don't — they belong to each instance:

- ❌ Kanban stage (where in the workflow), progress, subtask
  completion, due date for individual instances

So renaming a recurring series doesn't reset your progress on the
"this one" you're currently working on.

## When to use it

- Routine work: "submit weekly report", "triage the Ideas column every Monday"
- Maintenance: "rotate SSL certs every 90 days", "charge & log the battery bank weekly"
- Habits: "exercise plan, daily"

Don't use it for one-off work that just happens to repeat sometimes —
create those as new tasks.
