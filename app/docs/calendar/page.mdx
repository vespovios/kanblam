# Calendar

The Calendar view shows tasks laid out by **due date**, in either a
month grid or a week grid.

![The month calendar with Stratos-1 task pills and multi-day bars spread across launch month](/images/docs/calendar-month.png)

## Switching views

Top-left of the calendar:

- **Month** — six-week grid showing the visible month plus surrounding
  days. Each day cell shows compact pills for the tasks due that day.
- **Week** — single-week, tall day columns. Tasks with both a start
  date and a due date render as a horizontal **bar** spanning multiple
  days; single-day tasks render as pills.

Arrows take you back/forward by one period; **Today** snaps to the
current date.

![The week view with a multi-day task bar spanning several days above single-day pills](/images/docs/calendar-week.png)

## What renders where

- **Single-day tasks** (only `dueDate` set) → a compact pill on the
  due-date cell.
- **Multi-day tasks** (`startDate` and `dueDate` both set) → a bar
  spanning the date range. The bar's left edge is the start, the
  right edge is the due date. On the demo board, *"Design tracker
  PCB"* runs as a two-week bar while the one-day errands sit as
  pills beneath it.
- **Tasks without a due date** don't appear on the calendar at all.
  Use the [Tasks list](/docs/tasks) to see them.

## Pill colour and glyphs

Each pill shows:

- A priority dot at the left edge (high / medium / low)
- The task name (truncated if long — hover for the full text)
- Optional glyphs: ★ Important, ⏱ Urgent, 🔁 Recurring

## Dragging tasks across dates

Click and hold a pill → drag to another day → release. The task's
due date updates immediately. Multi-day bars work the same way; the
entire bar moves as a unit, preserving its length.

This makes the calendar the natural place for the morning "be honest
with yourself" pass: everything DayDash flags as overdue is one drag
away from a date you'll actually hit.

**Working days only** — if you've configured working days in
**Settings → Working days**, the calendar tints non-working days
slightly so you can see them at a glance.

## Holidays

Workspace admins can add holiday dates in **Settings → Holidays** —
individually, or a whole country's public holidays in one go with
the [bulk import](/docs/account#holidays). Holiday dates render with
a coloured strip; KanBlam treats them as non-working days for "due
today" / "overdue" computations on DayDash.

## Planning a deadline backwards

The week view earns its keep when you plan *backwards* from a fixed
date. Launch day is the 1st? Give "Run flight-path predictions" a
start date a week before and a due date the day before — it renders
as a bar you physically can't miss. Then drag the loose pills
(helium pickup, weather checks) into the gaps around it.
