# DayDash

DayDash is the page you land on after login — one screen that answers
"what needs me today?" before you open a single other view.

![DayDash showing overdue and due-today lists, stat cards, and the weekly workload chart for the Stratos-1 workspace](/images/docs/daydash.png)

## The stat cards

Four numbers across the top, computed from every task you can see
(they respect the global filter chips):

- **Overdue** — tasks whose due date is in the past and that aren't
  Completed or Cancelled. Working days and holidays are respected: a
  task due Friday isn't "overdue" on Saturday morning if your weekend
  isn't a working day.
- **Due today** — due dates matching today.
- **Due this week** — everything due in the current week.
- **Completion** — percentage of your tasks sitting in a terminal
  stage. The long-run "are we actually finishing things?" number.

## The action lists

Below the cards, three lists in priority order:

- **Overdue** — oldest first. This list is deliberately at the top:
  if something's rotting, DayDash won't let you scroll past it.
- **Due today** — your commitments for the day.
- **Q1 — Important + Urgent** — tasks flagged both important *and*
  urgent, regardless of date. If it's in this list and not in the
  other two, it's the thing you should be doing between deadlines.

Click any row to jump straight to that task.

## The charts

The lower half of DayDash is orientation, not action:

- **Tasks by stage** — how work is distributed across your Kanban
  columns. A fat "In Progress" bar with a thin "Completed" bar is the
  classic too-much-WIP signature.
- **The week ahead** — due-date load for each day of the current
  week, so Thursday doesn't ambush you on Thursday.
- **By priority** — the spread from Very High to Very Low.
- **Project progress** — one bar per project, driven by task
  completion in that project.
- **Recent activity** — the latest task changes across the workspace.
  With a team, this is your morning "what happened while I was out".

## A morning routine that works

1. Open DayDash (you're already there — it's the login landing page).
2. Clear or re-date everything in **Overdue**. An honest re-date beats
   a rotting red number.
3. Scan **Due today** and drag anything unrealistic to tomorrow in the
   [Calendar](/docs/calendar).
4. Pick the top task from **Q1** and go do it — that's the whole trick.
