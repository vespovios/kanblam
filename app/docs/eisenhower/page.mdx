# Eisenhower matrix

The Eisenhower matrix is a four-quadrant view sorting tasks by two
flags: **important** and **urgent**.

![The Eisenhower matrix with Stratos-1 tasks distributed across the Do, Schedule, Delegate, and Eliminate quadrants](/images/docs/eisenhower.png)

## The four quadrants

|  | Urgent | Not urgent |
|---|---|---|
| **Important** | **Do** — handle now | **Schedule** — plan for it |
| **Not important** | **Delegate** — to someone else | **Eliminate** — should you really be doing this? |

The point isn't moralism. It's that "important" and "urgent" are
*different* — and most people conflate them. Time spent in **Schedule**
(important, not urgent) is the time that compounds. Time spent in
**Delegate** and **Eliminate** is the time you should be reclaiming.

A concrete read of the demo board: *"Rent helium cylinder"* sits in
**Do** — the launch literally can't happen without it and the window
is days away. *"Write telemetry firmware"* is **Schedule** — mission
critical, but there are weeks to do it properly. *"Data SIMs for
chase phones"* is **Delegate** — someone has to, it needn't be you.
And *"Integrate camera module"* lives in **Eliminate** territory:
nice to have, quietly optional.

## Setting the flags

Each task has two checkboxes: **Important** and **Urgent**. Set them
in the task drawer (open any card to find them) or via Quick Add with
the `!important` and `!urgent` tokens:

```
Migrate analytics pipeline !important !urgent
```

That lands in **Do** (Q1) immediately.

## Moving tasks across quadrants

Drag a card from one quadrant to another — KanBlam flips the
underlying flags automatically. So "I changed my mind, this isn't
urgent anymore" is one drag.

**Keyboard:** tab to a card's grip → `Space` to pick up → arrow keys
nudge the card → `Space` drops on the quadrant under it.

Click anywhere on the card (other than the grip) to open it for full
editing.

## How Eisenhower interacts with Kanban

The two views show the **same tasks** filtered by different axes.
A task in Q1 (Important + Urgent) might also be sitting in your
Kanban "In Progress" column. Moving it on the Kanban doesn't change
its Eisenhower quadrant; moving it on Eisenhower doesn't change its
Kanban stage. Two independent dimensions.

## A weekly quadrant review

1. Open the matrix with no filters. If **Do** holds more than five
   tasks, some of them are lying — demote the pretenders.
2. Pick two **Schedule** tasks and give them start dates this week.
   Q2 work never becomes urgent *because* you did it early.
3. For each **Delegate** task, either assign it to someone or admit
   it's actually **Eliminate**.
4. Close the matrix and go do the top of **Do**.
